Data binding

Okay, so you should have a decent grasp on $scope at this point. Why don't we discuss this elusive binding thing. Whether you knew it or not, you have been working with data-binding throughout this tuitorial.

Lets start with a simple definition of what we're about to get into. According to the Angular docs: Data-binding in AngularJS apps is the automatic synchronization of data between the model and view components. The way that AngularJS implements data-binding lets you treat the model as the single-source-of-truth in your application. The view is a projection of the model at all times. When the model changes, the view reflects the change, and vice versa.

They even gave us a nifty diagram:

Let's make our student app a bit more interesting. We will bind an input field to the student.name attribute. This sets up bi-directional binding from the input field to the page. It is bi-directional here because if either the view or model see a change, it will be updated by AngularJS automatically for you. This is why our banking example worked without changing the hard coded data in our controller.

To get started, we'll use the ng-model function on our input, like so:

<div ng-controller="myFirstController">
  <input type="text" ng-model="person.name" placeholder="Enter your name" />
  <h5>Hello {{ student.name }}</h5>
</div>
Hello {{ student.name }}

Yes...that really is it. I know; seems too good to be true, but this is the magic of Angular!

Functions can also be called on the $scope. To do this, we would use the ng-click directive to bind a click event to a method. Why don't we make a simple counter to illustrate this: 

<div ng-controller="MathController">
  <h4>Yay Math!!</h4>
  <button ng-click="add(1)" class="button">Add</button>
  <button ng-click="subtract(1)" class="button">Subtract</button>
  <h4>Count: {{ counter }}</h4>
</div>

here it is:

Yay Math!!

Count: {{ counter }}

Please note that in the above example, we are telling Angular what method to call and the amount variable in parentheses. The entire value is treated as a string.

There are obviously more complicated examples of data-binding within Angualar, but for this tuitorials purposes we won't go any deeper. That said, it is one of the biggest reasons people like this framework. It can really speed up your process by doing some of the heavy lifting for you.
